## What Is OCR (Optical Character Recognition)?

OCR stands for **Optical Character Recognition** — the technology that reads text from images and converts it into editable, searchable text.

When you take a photo of a document, scan a receipt, or screenshot a webpage, the result is just an image. The computer sees pixels, not letters. OCR analyzes those pixels, recognizes patterns that match characters, and outputs actual text you can copy, edit, and search.

Modern OCR engines like **Tesseract** (the one powering this tool) use machine learning to recognize characters across dozens of languages with high accuracy.

## Tips for Better OCR Accuracy

The quality of your output depends heavily on the quality of your input. Here's how to get the best results:

### 1. Use High-Resolution Images
Low-resolution images (under 150 DPI) produce blurry characters that are hard to recognize. Aim for 300 DPI or higher for scanned documents. For photos, use your phone's highest quality setting.

### 2. Ensure Good Contrast
Black text on white background is ideal. Avoid images where text blends into the background. If your image has poor contrast, try adjusting brightness/contrast in any photo editor before uploading.

### 3. Keep the Image Straight
Tilted or skewed text reduces accuracy significantly. Most modern phones have a document scanner mode that automatically straightens images — use it.

### 4. Crop to the Text Area
If your image has a lot of non-text content (photos, decorations, borders), crop it down to just the text area before uploading. Less noise means better accuracy.

### 5. Avoid Decorative Fonts
Standard fonts (Arial, Times New Roman, Helvetica) are recognized with near-perfect accuracy. Highly stylized, handwritten, or decorative fonts are harder to read — even for humans.

### 6. Select the Right Language
Always select the language that matches your image. If your document is in Chinese, select Chinese. Mixing languages in one image can reduce accuracy.
