# How to Extract Text from a Screenshot (Free, No Software)

Got a screenshot with text you need to copy? Whether it's an error message, a social media post, or a document you can't select text from — there's a fast, free way to extract it.

## The Problem with Screenshots

Screenshots are images. The text inside them looks like text, but to your computer it's just colored pixels. You can't `Ctrl+C` it. You can't search it. You can't paste it into a document.

That's where OCR (Optical Character Recognition) comes in.

## The Fastest Way: Paste Directly

The quickest method requires zero extra steps:

1. Take your screenshot (`Ctrl+PrintScreen`, `Win+Shift+S`, or `Cmd+Shift+4` on Mac)
2. Go to [ImageToText](/)
3. Press `Ctrl+V` (or `Cmd+V`) to paste directly into the tool
4. The text is extracted in seconds

No saving files, no uploading — just paste and copy.

## Step-by-Step for Any Screenshot

If you already have a screenshot saved as a file:

1. Open [ImageToText](/)

3. Select the language (English is default)
4. Wait for processing — usually under 10 seconds
5. Copy the extracted text

## Best Practices for Screenshot OCR

**Capture at full resolution.** Don't resize or compress your screenshot before uploading. Higher resolution = better accuracy.

**Avoid partial screenshots of small text.** If the text is tiny in the original, zoom in before screenshotting, or use your OS's zoom feature.

**Dark mode screenshots work fine.** White text on dark background is recognized just as well as dark text on light background.

**Multiple columns of text.** If your screenshot has text in multiple columns (like a newspaper layout), the OCR may mix up the reading order. Crop each column separately for best results.

## Use Cases

- **Error messages** — copy error text from apps that don't allow text selection
- **Social media posts** — extract quotes from Twitter/X, Instagram, or WeChat screenshots
- **Video subtitles** — screenshot a frame and extract the subtitle text
- **Locked PDFs** — screenshot pages from PDFs that disable copy-paste
- **Chat history** — extract text from messaging app screenshots
- **Code screenshots** — recover code from screenshots shared in presentations

## Privacy Note

All processing happens in your browser. Your screenshots are never uploaded to any server. This is especially important for screenshots that might contain sensitive information like passwords, personal messages, or confidential documents.
