# How to Convert Image to Text on Your Phone (iOS & Android)

Need to extract text from an image on your phone? Whether you're on iPhone or Android, there are several fast, free ways to do it — no app download required.

## Method 1: Use ImageToText in Your Mobile Browser (Any Phone)

The easiest method works on any smartphone:

1. Open your phone's browser (Safari, Chrome, etc.)
2. Go to **imagetotext.top**
3. Tap the upload area and select your photo from the camera roll
4. Wait a few seconds for processing
5. Copy the extracted text

This works on iPhone, Android, and any other device with a modern browser. No app needed, no account required.

## Method 2: Google Lens (Android & iOS)

Google Lens is the fastest option if you want to capture text in real-time:

**On Android:**
- Open the Google app or Google Photos
- Tap the Lens icon (camera with a circle)
- Point at text or select a photo
- Tap the text to copy it

**On iPhone:**
- Download the Google app from the App Store
- Tap the Lens icon in the search bar
- Point at text or select a photo

Google Lens is excellent for real-time capture — point your camera at a menu, sign, or document and copy the text instantly.

## Method 3: Live Text (iPhone iOS 15+)

If you have an iPhone running iOS 15 or later, Live Text is built right in:

1. Open the **Photos** app
2. Find the photo with text
3. Tap and hold on the text in the photo
4. Select **Copy** from the menu

Live Text also works in the Camera app — just point at text and tap the Live Text icon in the corner.

**Limitation:** Live Text only works with Latin-based languages (English, Spanish, French, etc.) and a few others. For Chinese, Japanese, or Korean, use ImageToText or Google Lens instead.

## Method 4: Samsung Bixby Vision (Samsung Phones)

Samsung Galaxy phones have Bixby Vision built into the camera:

1. Open the Camera app
2. Tap the Bixby Vision icon
3. Select the **Text** mode
4. Point at text to extract it

## Tips for Mobile OCR

**Use good lighting.** Phone cameras struggle in low light. Take photos near a window or turn on a light.

**Hold steady.** Blurry photos are the #1 cause of poor OCR results. Use both hands or prop your phone against something.

**Use portrait mode carefully.** Portrait mode blurs the background, which is fine for OCR as long as the text itself is in focus.

**Crop before uploading.** Use your phone's built-in crop tool to remove unnecessary parts of the image before uploading to ImageToText.

## Conclusion

For most mobile OCR needs, you have two great free options: **Google Lens** for real-time capture and **ImageToText.top** for privacy-safe browser-based conversion. iPhone users on iOS 15+ also have the convenient built-in Live Text feature.
